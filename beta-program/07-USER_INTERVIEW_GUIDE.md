# User Interview Guide

## Interview Goals

1. Understand user's learning journey and motivations
2. Identify pain points and friction in the app
3. Discover what features resonate most
4. Gauge willingness to pay and price sensitivity
5. Uncover feature requests and gaps
6. Compare Langflix to competitor apps

**Target:** 10 interviews minimum (aim for 20)

---

## Interview Logistics

**Duration:** 30 minutes

**Format:** Video call (Zoom or Google Meet)

**Recording:** Always ask permission first
- "Do you mind if I record this? It helps me review later. The recording is just for internal use."

**Compensation:** $25 Amazon gift card (send within 24 hours)

---

## Pre-Interview Checklist

- [ ] Send calendar invite with Zoom/Meet link
- [ ] Reminder email 24 hours before
- [ ] Test recording setup
- [ ] Review user's survey responses beforehand
- [ ] Check their usage stats (days active, videos watched, etc.)

## Interview Script

### Introduction (2 minutes)

"Hi [Name]! Thanks so much for taking the time to chat. This is super casualâ€”I just want to hear about your experience with Langflix and learn how we can make it better.

There are no wrong answers, and please be brutally honest. I'd rather hear what sucks than have you be polite. Sound good?

---

### Section 1: Background & Motivation (5 minutes)

**1. Tell me about your Spanish learning journey.**
- When did you start learning?
- Why Spanish specifically?
- What's your goal? (travel, work, heritage, fun, etc.)

**2. What have you tried so far?**
- Which apps or methods?
- What worked? What didn't?
- Why did you stop using [previous app]?

**3. What made you want to try Langflix?**
- What in the beta signup post caught your attention?
- What were you hoping it would do differently?

**[LISTEN FOR: Motivations, pain points with existing solutions, expectations]**

---

### Section 2: First Impressions (5 minutes)

**4. Walk me through your first time opening Langflix.**
- What did you do first?
- What was your immediate reaction?
- Anything confusing or surprising?

**5. Did you understand how to use it?**
- Was onboarding clear?
- Did you discover features on your own or by accident?
- What did you wish you'd known upfront?

**6. What was your first "aha!" moment?**
- When did it click?
- What made you think "oh, this is cool!"?

**[LISTEN FOR: Onboarding friction, feature discovery, initial delight moments]**

---

### Section 3: Usage & Habits (7 minutes)

**7. How often have you been using Langflix?**
- Daily? Few times a week? Once?
- What time of day?
- For how long each session?

**8. Walk me through a typical session.**
- What do you do first?
- How many videos do you watch?
- Do you save words? Practice with AI chat?

**9. What makes you come back?**
- What's the hook?
- What feature do you use most?
- What keeps you engaged?

**10. Have you missed any days? If so, why?**
- Too busy? Lost interest? Technical issues?
- What would make you use it more consistently?

**[LISTEN FOR: Usage patterns, habit triggers, engagement drivers, barriers]**

---

### Section 4: Features Deep Dive (8 minutes)

**11. Let's talk about the video feed. Thoughts?**
- How's the content quality?
- Are videos at the right level for you?
- Do you feel like it's learning your preferences?
- What would make the feed better?

**12. What about the tap-to-translate feature?**
- How often do you use it?
- Does it work as expected?
- Any frustrations?

**13. Have you used the AI conversation partner?**
- If yes: What was that like? Would you use it again?
- If no: Why not? What would make you try it?

**14. Are you using the spaced repetition for vocab?**
- If yes: Is it helping you remember words?
- If no: Why not? Too complicated? Don't see the value?

**15. What's your favorite feature?**
- What do you find yourself using most?
- What would you be sad to lose?

**16. What's missing?**
- What feature do you wish existed?
- What would make this a daily habit for you?

**[LISTEN FOR: Feature usage, value perception, pain points, feature requests]**

---

### Section 5: Comparison & Competition (3 minutes)

**17. How does Langflix compare to [app they mentioned earlier]?**
- What's better? What's worse?
- Would you use both, or switch completely?

**18. If you could only use one language app, which would it be?**
- Why?

**[LISTEN FOR: Competitive positioning, unique value props, deal-breakers]**

---

### Section 6: Pricing & Value (5 minutes)

**19. Let's talk money. Would you pay for Langflix?**
- Why or why not?

**20. If yes: What would you expect to pay per month?**
- Don't tell them the priceâ€”let them suggest
- If they hesitate: "Just ballpark it. What feels fair?"

**21. What would make it worth $4.99/month?**
- What would need to exist or improve?
- What's the minimum viable product for that price?

**22. How does that compare to what you'd pay for Duolingo, Babbel, etc.?**
- Is language learning worth paying for?
- What makes an app worth subscribing to?

**[LISTEN FOR: Price sensitivity, value perception, purchase drivers, price anchors]**

---

### Section 7: Final Thoughts (3 minutes)

**23. If you were me, what would you prioritize?**
- What's the #1 thing to fix or improve?
- What's working that I should double down on?

**24. Would you recommend Langflix to a friend learning Spanish?**
- Why or why not?
- What would you tell them?

**25. Anything else I should know?**
- Any other feedback, ideas, or thoughts?

---

### Closing (2 minutes)

"This was incredibly helpful. Thank you so much for being honestâ€”this is exactly the feedback I need to make Langflix better.

I'll send you that $25 Amazon gift card within 24 hours.

And please keep using the app and filling out the daily surveys. If you ever have other thoughts, you can always reach me on Discord or email.

Thanks again!"

## Interview Selection Criteria

Who to interview:

### Must interview (4-5 people):
- Top 2 most active users (understand what drives engagement)
- Top 2 least active users who didn't churn (understand barriers)
- 1 user who churned (understand why)

### Should interview (5-10 people):
- Mix of Spanish levels (2 beginners, 3 intermediate, 2 advanced)
- Mix of devices (iOS and Android)
- Users with different use cases (travel, work, heritage, fun)
- Users who gave detailed survey responses
- Users with interesting feedback or bug reports

### Nice to have (5-10 people):
- Power users of specific features (AI chat, vocab, etc.)
- Users from different demographics (age, location)
- Users who compared to specific competitors
